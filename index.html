About

I am a final-year Mathematics student at Airlangga University, Surabaya, with a deep passion for algebra and mathematical analysis. My academic journey has been shaped by both rigorous theoretical study and active involvement in the mathematics community.

My current research centers on inequalities in real numbers, with my undergraduate thesis focusing on Discrete Steffensen's Inequality and Its Generalizations — a problem at the intersection of classical analysis and discrete mathematics.

Beyond research, I am deeply committed to mathematics education. I have tutored calculus, linear algebra, differential equations, and other courses to undergraduate students across multiple disciplines, and have served as a question designer for the Math and Logic Competition (MLC).

I aspire to pursue a master's degree in mathematics, where I can further investigate open problems in analysis and contribute to the mathematical community through both research and teaching.

Research & Publications

Undergraduate Thesis · In Progress
Discrete Steffensen's Inequality and Its Generalization

This research generalizes the classical discrete Steffensen's inequality using convex and concave function approaches on measure spaces. We prove an upper bound generalization for parameters \(\alpha, \beta\) with \(\beta \geq \alpha - 1\) and \(\alpha > 1\), and establish a new lower bound generalization for \(0 < \beta \leq 1 - \alpha\) with \(0 \leq \alpha < 1\), extending prior results of Gauchman via Jensen's inequality on Lebesgue spaces.

Co-authored with Eridani and Muchammad Yusuf Syaifuddin · Department of Mathematics, Universitas Airlangga

Academic Program · Selected Participant
3-Day School on Harmonic & Functional Analysis 2025

A competitive intensive program held at Institut Teknologi Bandung (ITB), Ganesha Campus, December 2025, devoted to the study of harmonic and functional analysis and related topics. The program featured 6 main lectures by Prof. Hendra Gunawan (ITB), complemented by two special lectures from invited lecturers.

Participants were selected undergraduate mathematics students from across Indonesian universities who had completed Multivariable Calculus and/or Real Analysis — with only 10 non-ITB seats available nationally. The program provided deep insights into research perspectives in modern mathematical analysis.
